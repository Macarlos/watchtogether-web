Legal & Privacy

Last updated: July 23, 2026

What Watch2Night is

Watch2Night is a free tool that helps you find something to watch. You pick your streaming platforms and preferred mood, swipe through suggestions, and save the ones you like. There's no account, no sign-up, and nothing to install.

By using Watch2Night, you accept these terms. If you don't agree with them, please don't use the site.

Cookies & local storage

As things stand today, Watch2Night uses no tracking cookies, no advertising cookies, and no analytics platforms (like Google Analytics). We don't sell or share data with advertisers, and there's nothing here trying to identify or follow you across sites. (See "Advertising & affiliate links" below for how this may change in the future.)

The only thing stored in your browser is local storage (a browser feature similar to cookies, used to remember information locally on your device) — specifically:

  • Your saved picks — so they're still there next time you visit. Cleared anytime via "Clear all picks" in the app, or your browser's own site-data settings.
  • Whether you've dismissed the cookie notice — so it doesn't reappear every visit.

Both are strictly necessary for features you directly use (remembering your own saved picks, not re-showing a notice you already closed) — under GDPR and the ePrivacy Directive, this kind of essential functional storage doesn't require opt-in consent, only disclosure, which is what this section is. Nothing here is used for tracking, profiling, or advertising.

We separately keep one anonymous, aggregate server-side counter of how many times the app has been opened and which platforms/genres get selected most — this is a simple running tally with no cookie, no identifier, and no way to connect it back to any individual visitor.

Languages & translation

Watch2Night's interface is available in English, Spanish, Portuguese, French, German, Hindi, and Polish, automatically shown based on your detected location.

For movie and show descriptions: English, Spanish, French, and German come directly from our data provider's own catalog. Portuguese, Hindi, and Polish descriptions are machine-translated using a third-party AI service (Groq), since our data provider doesn't offer these languages natively. Machine translation can occasionally be imperfect — the original English description is always the authoritative source if anything reads oddly.

Shared picks links

If you use the "Share my picks" feature, Watch2Night generates a link containing only the internal catalog IDs of the titles you liked — no personal information, no names, no identifying data of any kind. Anyone who opens that link can see which titles were included; nothing else about you is shared or stored.

Advertising & affiliate links

Watch2Night is currently free to use and does not show ads. Running the service has real ongoing costs, and to help support that, we may in the future introduce advertising and/or affiliate links — for example, earning a small commission if you choose to rent or buy a title through a link shown in the app, at no extra cost to you.

If advertising is introduced, our intention is for it to remain non-invasive, and it will not be used to build cross-site profiles of individual visitors or to sell your data to third parties. As with any change described in this notice, we'll update this page in advance — see "Changes to this page" below.

Where the data comes from

Movie and TV information — titles, posters, descriptions, ratings, and streaming availability — is provided by the Streaming Availability API by Movie of the Night, a third-party data provider, via their API. Watch2Night does not own, verify, or guarantee the accuracy of this data. Trailer links point to a YouTube search for the title, not a specific verified video.

Availability and pricing is shown for one of eleven supported countries — United States, Canada, India, United Kingdom, Australia, Mexico, Spain, Brazil, France, Germany, or Poland — automatically detected from your connection. If you're browsing from anywhere else, you'll see the US catalog by default. Watch2Night's data plan only supports accurate lookups for these countries, so if your detected country is wrong (for example, due to a VPN) or you're located elsewhere, what you see may not match what's actually offered on your local version of these platforms. Always confirm current availability and price directly on the streaming platform before making a purchase or rental decision.
